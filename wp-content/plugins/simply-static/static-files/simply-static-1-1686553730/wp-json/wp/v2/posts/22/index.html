On Saturday night in Istanbul, as the referee blew the full time whistle, Manchester City players burst into a wild celebration. Why wouldn\u2019t they! The team that was always considered the \u2018second\u2019 club of Manchester had just emulated their big brothers, becoming the first English club since Manchester United in 1999 to win the treble\u2013 The Premier League, the FA Cup and the Champions League.<\/p>\n

As the players celebrated on the field and the thousands of City fans rejoiced in the stands, the man behind all of it, the mastermind of the club, the \u2018genius\u2019 coach Pep Guardiola looked a bit subdued.<\/p>\n

He didn\u2019t run to his players after the final whistle like the substitutes and other coaches. He wasn\u2019t jumping up and down in elation nor was he in any rush to join the celebration.<\/p>\n

As the camera panned to Guardiola, which it had done multiple times during the match when he was pacing to and fro in the technical area restlessly, shouting instructions to his players and looking animated whenever his team survived a close call. Guardiola had just accomplished something he had been trying to achieve for years. And after getting the job done, he was seemingly overcome with an overwhelming sense of relief.<\/p>\n

Winning the Champions League is not something new for Guardiola, he had done it twice before with Barcelona. But for many reasons, the Champions League triumph with Manchester City is extra special.<\/p>\n

In the seven years since joining Manchester City, Guardiola has helped the club win every title there is to win in England.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n

Under Guardiola, Manchester City have won five Premier League titles, including a hattrick of title wins. They have also won the FA Cup twice, the League Cup four times and the Community Shield twice.<\/p>\n

Guardiola has also given the side a unique identity on the field, something its previous coaches had failed to do. The Spaniard is one of the biggest football philosophers in modern football and is also, perhaps, the best tactician in the game.<\/p>\n

Still, his success in Manchester City and his achievements at Barcelona and Bayern Munich are sometimes looked down upon by naysayers, who claim that Guardiola benefited from having great teams at his disposals and had little to do with his teams\u2019 achievements.<\/p>\n

After that, Guardiola arrived at Manchester City, a club which had tremendous financial backing since the Abu Dhabi United Group took over in 2008, but had only one Premier League title and an FA Cup win to show for.<\/p>\n

It took Guardiola one season to size up the league and to build his team. After that, began the domination of Manchester City. Clubs like Liverpool, Chelsea, Manchester United and Arsenal gave them competition, with Liverpool managing to win one Premier League title in between Man City\u2019s reign of excellence. The way Guardiola transformed a formidable team into a near unbeatable one was incredible. But the critics were quick to point out the massive financing behind the club, implying that it was the money that won Manchester City the trophies, not Guardiola.<\/p>\n

But they forgot a famous quote made by Guardiola\u2019s mentor, Barcelona\u2019s Dutch legend Johan Cruyff, \u201cI\u2019ve never seen a bag of money score a goal.\u201d<\/p>\n

Guardiola did have a lot of money at his disposal to go for the players he wanted to, but he wasn\u2019t the only one. The other English giants have also spent exuberant amount of money in this period. Still, they failed to match up with Manchester City.<\/p>\n

Guardiola knew the only way to shut the mouths of critics is to do the one trophy Manchester City, a club that has been around since 1880, had never done. Win the Champions League.<\/p>\n

Guardiola is also well aware that his time with Manchester City will be judged by his success in the Champions League, \u201cIt doesn\u2019t mean I agree with that but absolutely we will be judged by this competition,\u201d Guardiola told the media at a press conference.<\/p>\n
