Asia Cup matches involving India could be played in Sri Lanka after the cricketing powerhouse refused to visit arch-rival and official host Pakistan, a board official said Sunday.<\/p>\n

The Asian Cricket Council (ACC) is due to make a formal announcement in the coming week after calls for a different venue for India\u2019s games in the 50-over tournament in September, a Sri Lankan official said.<\/p>\n

Neighbours India and Pakistan have not met on either side\u2019s soil in any version of the game since 2012, and only play each other in international tournaments on neutral grounds.<\/p>\n

The two cricketing powerhouses have fought three wars since being carved out of the subcontinent\u2019s partition in 1947, and are bitter political rivals.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt is very likely that some of the matches will be in Sri Lanka,\u201d the board official said, asking not to be named.<\/p>\n

\u201cBut we have still not officially received confirmation.\u201d<\/p>\n

Sri Lanka\u2019s board had expressed willingness to host some Asia Cup matches, despite an economic crisis which forced it to default on its $46 billion foreign debt in April last year and seek a bailout from the International Monetary Fund.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n
